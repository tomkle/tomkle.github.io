Route 66

21 Days. 8 States. 1 Legend.

Crossing the finish line at Santa Monica Pier after 21 days is more than just finishing a drive. It’s a slow-motion study of the American heartland. Here is the blueprint of my three-week journey across the Mother Road.

Total Miles: 2,448 Coffee Consumed: 64 Cups States Crossed: 8
Days 1–5
The Heartland (IL & MO)

Starting in **Chicago**, the first few days were a blur of neon and cornfields. We explored the deep history of Abraham Lincoln in **Springfield, IL** before crossing the mighty Mississippi via the Chain of Rocks Bridge.

[Photo: The "Begin" Sign in Downtown Chicago]
Top Stop: Meramec Caverns (MO) — An incredible underground "city" where Jesse James once hid. The cool 58°F air was a gift.
Days 6–10
The Great Plains (OK & TX)

Oklahoma has more driveable miles of the original road than any other state. We saw the **Blue Whale of Catoosa** and hit the "Midpoint" of the entire route in **Adrian, Texas**. The sky opened up, and the horizons became infinite.

Top Stop: Cadillac Ranch (Amarillo, TX). We emptied four cans of neon spray paint on the half-buried cars. It’s messy, loud, and essential.
Days 11–16
High Desert & Canyons (NM & AZ)

New Mexico brought Adobe architecture and turquoise jewelry. We took a 2-day detour to the **Grand Canyon**, which is a rite of passage for any 66 traveler. Driving through the **Petrified Forest** felt like being on another planet.

[Photo: Sunset at the Grand Canyon South Rim]
Top Stop: Wigwam Motel (Holbrook, AZ). Yes, we actually slept in a concrete teepee. It’s kitschy, cozy, and 100% Route 66.
Days 17–21
The Final Stretch (CA)

The Mojave Desert is beautiful but brutal. We passed through the ghost town of **Amboy** and saw the Bottle Tree Ranch. Finally, the salt air hit us as we rolled into **Santa Monica** to touch the Pacific.

The Grand Finale: Standing under the "End of the Trail" sign at the pier. There were definitely some tears behind my sunglasses.
