The Mother Road: Chicago to Santa Monica

The Ultimate American Road Trip

Route 66, also known as "The Mother Road," stretches 2,448 miles from Chicago, Illinois to Santa Monica, California. Established in 1926, it's one of the most iconic highways in America and remains a symbol of adventure and freedom.

Iconic Stops Along the Way

Chicago, Illinois - Start your journey at the famous Route 66 sign on Adams Street. Don't miss the Art Institute and Navy Pier.
St. Louis, Missouri - Stop by the Gateway Arch, a stunning monument to westward expansion. Visit the City Museum for quirky underground adventures.
Oklahoma City, Oklahoma - Explore the Route 66 Museum and grab authentic BBQ. The city has a rich automotive heritage.
Amarillo, Texas - Visit the famous Cadillac Ranch where visitors spray paint vintage cars. A true Route 66 experience!
Albuquerque, New Mexico - The longest stretch of the original Route 66 runs through this vibrant city. Explore Old Town and stunning desert landscapes.
Flagstaff, Arizona - Located near the Grand Canyon, Flagstaff offers cool mountain air and historic Route 66 landmarks.
Barstow, California - The gateway to California features the Route 66 Mother Road Museum with vintage memorabilia.
Santa Monica, California - End your journey at the Santa Monica Pier, where Route 66 meets the Pacific Ocean. A true American milestone!

Trip Tips

Plan for at least 2-3 weeks to truly experience Route 66. Bring a camera, comfortable shoes, and an open mind. Stay in vintage motels, try local diners, and take the scenic routes. The journey is more important than the destination!
